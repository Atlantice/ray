Project RayFugee

Project RayFugee

In 2015, over 400,000 refugees applied for asylum in Germany alone. Those capable enough to endure the arduous trek by foot through Greece or the dangerous voyage over the Mediterranean Sea eventually reside in temporary living camps waiting to be placed into federal housing. The downsides of such living arrangements, is that skilled refugees are unable to exercise their skills productively. This has been the root cause of many issues, including low economic prospects, feelings of inadequacy, and wasted human potential.

Project Ray seeks to reintroduce economic opportunity, by empowering refugees in temporary camps to serve as entrepreneurs. Through our web based platform will match skilled refugees to equivalent job opportunities. As a result, these refugees will exercise their abilities in the countries to which they immigrate, and these nations will benefit from the expertise refugees bring in. We plan to enable low skilled refugees (or as we call them RayMakers), by producing an online peer-to-peer marketplace that connects refugee artisans and craftsman to local suppliers, and offers a place for them to sell their goods to the global market. We will accomplish this by producing an online platform that lists local suppliers, and crowdfunds the resources to purchase these supplies. These are then transported to refugee camps, where the RayMakers are able to produce unique handmade crafts (clothing, jewelry, pottery, paintings, etc). The RayMakers can use our marketplace to sell their goods.

Our business model is based on shared success; we make money when the RayMakers and the local suppliers make money. We will obtain our revenue from a $0.20 USD listing fee per item, and a 5% commision for every sold item. Shoppers can also choose to donate money at each transaction, where 80% will go to the RayMaker themselves, and 20% will be put into the Rays Me Up Foundation. The Rays Me Up Foundation will use this money to otherwise benefit the Refugee population (scholarships, classes, seminars, etc.).

Our current progress has been contacting several potential partners in Germany to begin developing a diversified list of industry contacts. Among this list include professionals in the medical and engineering industries, smaller non-profits working with refugees, human rights organizations, universities, and government agencies.
